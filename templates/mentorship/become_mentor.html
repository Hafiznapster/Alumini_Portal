About Becoming a Mentor

As a mentor, you'll have the opportunity to guide fellow alumni who are earlier in their career journey. Share your knowledge, experience, and insights to help them navigate their professional path.

Describe your professional experience, expertise, and how you believe you can help mentees. This will be visible to potential mentees.

Mentorship Guidelines
Mentor Responsibilities:
  • Commit to meeting with your mentee regularly as agreed upon
  • Provide honest, constructive feedback and guidance
  • Respect your mentee's confidentiality
  • Share relevant experiences and insights from your career journey
  • Help your mentee set realistic goals and provide support in achieving them
  • Communicate openly and clearly, responding to mentee inquiries in a timely manner
  • Provide networking opportunities when appropriate
Code of Conduct:
  • Maintain professional boundaries in all interactions
  • Treat all mentees with respect, regardless of background or experience level
  • Do not use the mentorship program for personal gain or to promote personal business interests
  • If you cannot fulfill your commitment, communicate this clearly to your mentee and the program administrators
  • Provide feedback to improve the mentorship program when requested
